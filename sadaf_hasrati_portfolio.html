what I do

Services I offer

Whether you need a full research pipeline built from scratch, help with a specific analysis, or a paper written — I can help. Here's what I specialise in.

🔬

Gene & disease analysis

I analyse large biological datasets to find which genes are active or inactive in a disease. I identify patterns, filter out noise, and highlight the genes that matter most for your research question.

💊

Drug target discovery

I map how drugs or natural compounds connect to disease-related genes — identifying which targets a compound hits, whether two compounds work together, and how they interact at the molecular level.

🤖

Machine learning for biomarkers

I use multiple AI algorithms to rank and validate the most promising disease markers from thousands of candidates. The result is a short, reliable list of genes that can distinguish diseased from healthy samples.

⚗️

Computational drug-target testing

I simulate how a drug molecule physically docks onto its target protein, and run extended simulations to confirm the binding holds over time. All done computationally — no lab needed.

🕸️

Biological network mapping

I build visual networks showing how genes, proteins, and compounds connect to each other and to disease. These maps make complex biology readable for both researchers and non-specialists.

✍️

Scientific writing

I write clear, structured research content — methods sections, literature reviews, mechanistic summaries, and figure descriptions. I have a peer-reviewed publication and international conference presentation to my name.

how I work

From raw data to ready results

Every project I take on follows a clear, structured process. You always know where things stand.

Step 1

Collect & clean data

I gather publicly available or client-provided biological datasets and prepare them for analysis — removing noise and making sure the data is reliable.

Step 2

Find the meaningful signals

I compare disease vs healthy samples to identify which genes behave differently and group them into meaningful clusters.

Step 3

Rank the best candidates

Three AI algorithms vote on which genes are truly important. Only the ones all three agree on make the final list — reducing false positives significantly.

Step 4

Map drug connections

I connect compound targets to the disease gene list, build interaction networks, and identify which biological pathways are affected.

Step 5

Test drug binding

I simulate the physical docking of drug molecules onto protein targets, scoring how well each compound fits.

Step 6

Confirm binding stability

I run extended molecular simulations to confirm that the binding holds under realistic conditions — not just a momentary fit.

Step 7

Validate independently

I test the findings on separate datasets the model has never seen, to confirm the results are real and not just an artefact of the training data.

Step 8

Deliver & explain

You receive clean figures, a written summary, and a clear explanation of what the results mean — ready for publication or presentation.

Finding shared disease genes and testing natural compound treatments — computationally

Completed

Two neurological conditions often occur together in patients. I investigated why — by analysing thousands of genes across multiple patient datasets to find the ones that are altered in both conditions simultaneously. I then asked: do certain natural compounds target exactly those shared genes? Using drug-target databases and computational simulations, I tested whether a two-compound combination would bind to those targets and hold stable — providing a scientific basis for the combination's potential effectiveness.

What I used →

public patient gene datasets statistical gene analysis 3 AI algorithms for gene ranking drug-target network mapping molecular docking simulation extended stability simulation AI explainability tools

Actual outputs from this project: gene expression patterns, overlap between the two diseases and compound targets, pathway analysis, and protein interaction network.
